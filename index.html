26.The Kuba Tonmöbel Company – Kuba-Tonmöbel

Brand names such as Traviata and Komet represent the part of the economic miracle that was “made in Wolfenbüttel”. In 1948, Gerhard Kubetschek founded the Kuba Tonmöbel (literally “sound furniture”) company with the idea incorporating radios, phonographs and later television sets into stylish furniture. He became one of the most successful West German entrepreneurs of the era. Consoles from Wolfenbüttel made record profits on the domestic and export markets. In 1966, Gerhard Kubetschek sold his business to General Electric for 80 million Deutschmarks. In 1972 the production facilities on Lindener Strasse closed.
